Let \(R\) be a ring, and \(I\) be an ideal. For \(f\in R\text{,}\)[^1]
\begin{equation*} V(I) \subseteq V(f) \Longleftrightarrow f\in \sqrt{I}. \end{equation*}
Moreover[^2]
\begin{equation*} \sqrt{I} = \bigcap_{P \in V(I)} P = \bigcap_{P \in \Min(I)} P. \end{equation*}
##### Proof. First to justify the equivalence of the two statements we observe:
\begin{equation*} V(I) \subseteq V(f) \Longleftrightarrow f\in P \ \text{for all} \ P \in V(I) \Longleftrightarrow f \in \bigcap_{P \in V(I)} P. \end{equation*}
