You may recall the Fundamental Theorem of Symmetric Polynomials says that every element of \(R^{S_n}\) can be written as polynomial expression in the elementary symmetric polynomials More precisely, \(R^{S_n} = k[e_1, \ldots, e_n]\text{.}\) For example, \(f\) above is \(e_1^2-2 e_2\text{.}\) In fact, any symmetric polynomial can be written like so in a way, so \(R^{S_n}\) is a free \(k\)-algebra. So even though we have infinitely many invariant polynomials, we can understand them in terms of only finitely many of them, which are invariants.
