First, note that any element of \(k[x,\frac{1}{x}]\) can be written in the form \(\frac{f(x)}{x^n}\) for some \(f \in k[x]\) and some \(n \geqslant 0\text{.}\) Now any finitely generated \(R\)-submodule of \(S\) is of the form
\begin{equation*} M = \sum_i R \cdot \frac{f_i(x)}{x^{n_i}} = \sum_i k[x] \cdot \frac{f_i(x)}{x^{n_i}}. \end{equation*}
If \(n := \max \{ n_i \}_i\text{,}\) then \(M \subseteq \frac{1}{x^n} k[x] \neq k[x,\frac{1}{x}] = S\text{.}\)
