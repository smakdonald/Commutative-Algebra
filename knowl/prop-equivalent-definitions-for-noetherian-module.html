Proposition 1.86. Equivalent Definitions for Noetherian Module.

Let \(M\) be an \(R\)-module. The following are equivalent: (a) \(M\) is a noetherian module. (b) Every nonempty family of submodules has a maximal element. (c) Every ascending chain of finitely generated submodules of \(M\) eventually stabilizes. (d) Given any generating set \(S\) for a submodule \(N\text{,}\) the submodule \(N\) is generated by a finite subset of \(S\text{.}\) (e) Every submodule of \(M\) is finitely generated. In particular, a noetherian module must be finitely generated.
