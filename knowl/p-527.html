Even if \(R\) is not noetherian, \(M = 0\) implies \(\Ass(M) = \es\) by definition[^3]. So we focus on the case when \(M \neq 0\text{.}\) If \(M \neq 0\text{,}\) then \(M\) contains a nonzero element \(m\text{,}\) and \(\ann(m)\) is contained in an associated prime of \(M\)[^2]. In particular, \(\Ass(M) \neq 0\text{.}\) In the graded setting, [[Mathematics/Commutative Algebra/Results/Lemma - Every Ann Contained in Ass Prime|Lemma]] gives us a homogeneous associated prime.
