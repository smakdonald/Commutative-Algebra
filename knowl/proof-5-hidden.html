Let \(S = R f_1 + \cdots + R f_n\text{,}\)[^2] meaning that \(f_1, \ldots, f_n\) is a set of module generators for \(S\) over \(R\text{.}\) Note that every \(R\)-linear combination of \(f_1, \ldots, f_n\) is also an element of \(R[f_1, \ldots, f_n]\text{,}\) and thus \(S\) is a subalgebra of \(R[f_1, \ldots, f_n]\text{.}\)
On the other hand, since \(f_1, \ldots, f_n \in S\) and \(S\) is an \(R\)-algebra, every polynomial in \(f_1, \ldots, f_n\) with coefficients in \(R\) is also in \(S\text{,}\) and thus \(S = R[f_1, \ldots, f_n]\text{,}\) so that \(S\) is algebra-finite over \(R\text{.}\)
