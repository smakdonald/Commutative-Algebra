Example 6.5. Minimal Primes of \((x^2,xy)\).

Let \(k\) be a field and \(R = k[x,y]\text{.}\) Every prime containing \(I = (x^2, xy)\) must contain \(x^2\text{,}\) and thus \(x\text{.}\) On the other hand, \((x)\) is a prime ideal containing \(I\text{.}\) Therefore, \(I\) has a unique minimal prime, and \(\Min(I) = \{ (x) \}\)[^1].
