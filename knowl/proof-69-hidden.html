Conversely, suppose that \(P_P = \ann(\frac{m}{w})\)[^3] for some \(\frac{m}{w} \in M_P\text{.}\) Let \(P = (f_1, \ldots, f_n)\text{.}\) For each \(i\text{,}\) since \(\frac{f_i}{1} \frac{m}{w} = \frac{0}{1}\text{,}\) there exists \(u_i \notin P\) such that \(u_i f_i m = 0\text{.}\) Then \(u = u_1 \cdots u_n\) is not in \(P\text{,}\) since \(P\) is prime, and \(u f_i m = 0\) for all \(i\text{.}\) Since the \(f_i\) generate \(P\text{,}\) we have \(P (um) = 0\text{.}\) On the other hand, if \(r \in \ann(um)\text{,}\) then \(\frac{ru}{1} \in \ann(\frac{m}{w}) = P_P\text{.}\) We conclude that \(ru \in P_P \cap R = P\text{.}\) Since \(u \notin P\) and \(P\) is prime, we conclude that \(r \in P\text{.}\)
