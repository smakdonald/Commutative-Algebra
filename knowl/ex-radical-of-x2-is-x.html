Example 3.30. Radical of \((x^2)\) is \((x)\).

Let \(k\) be a field, \(R = k[x]\)[^1], and \(I = (x^2)\text{.}\) On the one hand, it is immediate form the definition that \(x \in \sqrt{I}\text{,}\)[^2] and thus \((x) \subseteq \sqrt{I}\text{.}\) On the other hand, \((x)\) is a prime ideal that contains \(I\text{,}\) and thus by [[Mathematics/Commutative Algebra/Results/Theorem - Spectrum Analogue of Strong Nullstellensatz|Theorem]] we must have \(\sqrt{I} = (x)\text{.}\)
