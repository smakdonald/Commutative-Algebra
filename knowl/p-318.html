Let \(R\) be a ring, \(P_1, \ldots, P_n\) prime ideals, \(x \in R\) and \(I\) be an ideal in \(R\text{.}\) If \((x) + I \not\subseteq P_i\) for each \(i\text{,}\) then there exists \(y \in J\) such that
\begin{equation*} x + y \notin \bigcup_{i=1}^n P_i. \end{equation*}
##### Proof. We proceed by induction on \(n\text{.}\) When \(n=1\text{,}\) if every element of the form \(x+y\) with \(y \in R\) is in \(P= P_1\text{,}\) then multiplying by \(r \in R\) we conclude that every \(rx + y \in P\text{,}\) meaning \((x) + I \subseteq P\text{.}\)
