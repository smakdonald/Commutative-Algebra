Example 4.24. Exercise -.

For any field \(k\text{,}\) there is a contravariant functor from affine varieties over \(k\) to affine \(k\)-algebras that (a) on objects, maps a variety \(X\) to its coordinate ring \(k[X]\text{,}\) (b) on morphisms, maps a morphism of varieties \(X \xrightarrow{\phi} Y\) to its pullback \(k[Y] \xrightarrow{\phi^*} k[X]\text{.}\)
