Example 1.43. Integers are a Normal Domain.

The ring of integers \(\mathbb{Z}\) is a integrally closed, meaning its integral closure in its fraction field \(\mathbb{Q}\) is \(\mathbb{Z}\) itself. The key idea to show this is similar to the argument we used [provisional cross-reference: cite] [[Mathematics/Commutative Algebra/Results/Exercise - \(frac{1}{2}\) in \(Q\) Not Integral in \(Z\)|here]] to show that \(\frac{1}{2}\) is not integral over \(\Z\text{.}\)
