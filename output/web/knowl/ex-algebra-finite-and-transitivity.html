Example 1.37. Algebra Finite and Transitivity.

Let \(A\subseteq B \subseteq C\) be rings. It follows from the definitions that
\begin{equation*} \text{(a)}\quad\quad\quad\begin{align*}\\ &A\subseteq B \,\,\text{algebra-finite}\\ &\quad \quad \quad \quad \text{and}\\ &B\subseteq C \,\,\text{algebra-finite} \end{align*} \Longrightarrow \hspace{2em} A\subseteq C \,\,\text{algebra-finite} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{(b)}\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad A\subseteq C \,\text{algebra-finite} \Longrightarrow B\subseteq C \,\text{algebra-finite}. \end{equation*}
