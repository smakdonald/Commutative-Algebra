Definition 1.7. Homology.

The homology of the complex \(\left(C_{\bullet}, \delta_{\bullet}\right)\) is the sequence of \(R\)-modules
\begin{equation*} \mathrm{H}_{n}\left(C_{\bullet}\right):=\frac{\operatorname{ker} \delta_{n}}{\operatorname{im} \delta_{n+1}} . \end{equation*}
The \(n\)th homology of \(\left(C_{\bullet}, \delta_{\bullet}\right)\) is \(\mathrm{H}_{n}\left(C_{\bullet}\right)\text{.}\) The submodules \(Z_{n}\left(C_{\bullet}\right):=\operatorname{ker} \delta_{n} \subseteq C_{n}\) are called cycles, while the submodules \(B_{n}\left(C_{\bullet}\right):=\operatorname{im} \delta_{n+1} \subseteq C_{n}\) are called boundaries.
