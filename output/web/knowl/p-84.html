Let \(A\subseteq B \subseteq C\) be rings. It follows from the definitions that
  1. If \(A\subseteq B\) algebra-finite and \(B\subseteq C\) algebra finite, then \(A\subseteq C\) algebra finite.
  2. \(\displaystyle A\subseteq C \,\text{algebra-finite} \Longrightarrow B\subseteq C \,\text{algebra-finite}.\)
