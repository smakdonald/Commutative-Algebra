(c) Consider \(\A^6_k\) as the space of \(2 \times 3\) matrices over \(K\) with coordinates \(x_{11},\dots,x_{23}\text{,}\) and consider \(\A^5_K\) as the space of pairs of \(2 \times 1\) and \(1\times 3\) matrices over \(K\) with coordinates \(y_1,y_2, z_1,z_2,z_3\text{.}\) The map of matrix multiplication from \(\A^5_k\) to \(\A^6_k\) is a regular map:
\begin{equation*} (y_1,y_2,z_1,z_2,z_3) \mapsto \begin{bmatrix} y_1 z_1 & y_1 z_2 & y_1 z_3 \\ y_2 z_1 & y_2 z_2 & y_2 z_3 \end{bmatrix}. \end{equation*}
