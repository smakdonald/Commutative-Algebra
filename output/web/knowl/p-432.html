Let \((R,\fm,k)\) be a local ring. Then one of the following holds: (a) \(\char(R)=\char(k)=0\text{.}\) We say that \(R\) has equal characteristic zero. (b) \(\char(R)=0\text{,}\) \(\char(k)=p\) for a prime \(p\text{,}\) so \(R\) has mixed characteristic \((0,p)\) (c) \(\char(R)=\char(k)=p\) for a prime \(p\text{,}\) so \(R\) has equal characteristic \(p\text{.}\) (d) \(\char(R)=p^n\text{,}\) \(\char(k)=p\) for a prime \(p\) and an integer \(n>1\text{.}\) If \(R\) is reduced, then one of the first three cases holds.
