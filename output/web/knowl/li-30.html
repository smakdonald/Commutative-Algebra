Item 1.

Let \(r\) be integral over \(A\text{,}\) with \(r^n + a_{n-1} r^{n-1} + \cdots + a_1 r + a_0 = 0\) for some \(a_i \in A\text{.}\) We claim that \(A[r] = A + Ar + \cdots + A r^{n-1}\text{.}\) Since \(A[r]\) is generated by all the powers \(r^m\) of \(r\) as an \(A\)-module, to show that any polynomial \(p(r) \in A[r]\) is in \(A + Ar + \cdots + A r^{n-1}\) it is enough to show that \(r^m \in A + Ar + \cdots + A r^{n-1}\) for all \(m\text{.}\)
