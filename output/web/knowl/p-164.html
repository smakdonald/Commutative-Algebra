As a special case of the previous example, consider the ring of germs of complex analytic functions near $0$, \index{$\CC\{z\}$}
\begin{equation*} \CC\{z\} := \{ f(z) \in \CC\llbracket z \rrbracket \ | \ f \text{ is analytic on a neighborhood of }z=0\}. \end{equation*}
This ring is a PID: every ideal is of the form \((z^n)\text{,}\) since any \(f\in \CC\{z\}\) can be written as \(z^n g(z)\) for some \(g(z)\neq 0\text{,}\) and any such \(g(z)\) is a unit in \(\CC\{z\}\text{.}\)
