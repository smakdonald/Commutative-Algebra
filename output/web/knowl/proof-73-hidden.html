Even if \(R\) is not noetherian, \(M = 0\) implies \(\Ass(M) = \es\) by definition[^3]. So we focus on the case when \(M \neq 0\text{.}\) If \(M \neq 0\text{,}\) then \(M\) contains a nonzero element \(m\text{,}\) and \(\ann(m)\) is contained in an associated prime of \(M\)[^2]. In particular, \(\Ass(M) \neq 0\text{.}\) In the graded setting, [[Mathematics/Commutative Algebra/Results/Lemma - Every Ann Contained in Ass Prime|Lemma]] gives us a homogeneous associated prime.
Zerodivisors Associated Primes ##### Statement If \(R\) is noetherian, and \(M\) is an arbitrary \(R\)-module, then[^1]
\begin{equation*} \bigcup\limits_{P \in \Ass(M)} P = \mathcal{Z}(M). \end{equation*}
##### Proof. If \(r \in \mathcal{Z}(M)\)[^3], then by definition we have \(r \in \ann(m)\)[^6] for some nonzero \(m \in M\)[^4]. Since \(\ann(m)\) is contained in some associated prime of \(M\)[^2], then \(r\) is also contained in some associated prime of \(M\)[^5]. On the other hand, if \(P\) is an associated prime of \(M\text{,}\) then by definition all elements in \(P\) are zerodivisors on \(M\text{.}\)
For the graded case, replace the set of zerodivisors with the annihilators of homogeneous elements. Such annihilator is homogeneous, since if \(m\) is homogeneous, and \(fm=0\text{,}\) writing \(f=f_{a_1}+\dots+f_{a_b}\) as a sum of homogeneous elements of different degrees \(a_i\text{,}\) then \(0=fm=f_{a_1}m+\dots+f_{a_b}m\) is a sum of homogeneous elements of different degrees, so \(f_{a_i}m=0\) for each \(i\text{.}\)
