Remark 2.20.

There are many interesting examples of \(\N\)-graded algebras with \(R_0 = k\text{;}\) in that case, \(R_+\) is the largest homogeneous ideal in \(R\text{.}\) In fact, \(R_0\) is the only maximal ideal of \(R\) that is also homogeneous, so we can call it {}; it is sometimes also called the {} of \(R\text{.}\) This ideal plays a very important role: in many ways, \(R\) and \(R_+\) behave similarly to a local ring \(R\) and its unique maximal ideal. We will discuss this further when we learn about local rings.
