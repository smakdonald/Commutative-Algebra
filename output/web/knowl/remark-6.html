Remark 1.36.

Let \(A\subseteq B \subseteq C\) be rings. It follows from the definitions that
  1. If \(A\subseteq B\) module-finite and \(B\subseteq C\) module-finite, then \(A\subseteq C\) module-finite.
  2. \(\displaystyle A\subseteq C \,\text{module-finite} \Longrightarrow B\subseteq C \,\text{module-finite}.\)
