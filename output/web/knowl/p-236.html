If \(k\) is a field, and \(R=k[x_1,\dots,x_n]\) is a polynomial ring, we can give different \(\N\)-gradings on \(R\) by fixing some tuple \((\beta_1,\dots,\beta_n)\in \N^n\) and letting \(x_i\) be a homogeneous element of degree \(\beta_i\text{;}\) we call this a grading with \(\defnn{weights}\) \((\beta_1,\dots,\beta_n)\text{.}\) For example, in \(k[x_1,x_2]\text{,}\) \(x_1^2+x_2^3\) is not homogeneous in the standard grading, but it is homogeneous of degree \(6\) under the \(\mathbb{N}\)-grading with weights \((3,2)\text{.}\)
