Generalize the last example as follows: let \(k\) be a field with a primitive \(d\)th root of unity \(\zeta\text{,}\) and let \(G= \langle g \rangle \cong C_d\) act on \(R = k[x_1,\dots,x_n]\) via \(g \cdot x_i = \zeta x_i\) for all \(i\text{.}\) Then
\begin{equation*} R^G = \{ f \in R \ | \ \text{every term of $f$ has degree a multiple of $d$}\} = k[\{ \text{monomials of degree d}\}]. \end{equation*}
This what is known as the Veronese subring of \(R\) of degree \(d\text{.}\)
