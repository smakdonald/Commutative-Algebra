Remark 1.45.

We cannot talk about the integral closure of a ring \(R\) without specifying in what extension; the integral closures of \(R\) in different extension can be very different. In , we saw that the integral closure of \(\Z\) in \(\Z[\sqrt{2}]\) contains at least \(\Z\) and \(\sqrt{2}\text{,}\) while this [provisional cross-reference: cite] [[Mathematics/Commutative Algebra/Results/Exercise - Integers are a Normal Domain|Exercise]] says that the integral closure of \(\Z\) in \(\Q\) is \(\Z\text{.}\)
When \(R\) is a domain, if we ever refer to the integral closure of \(R\text{,}\) it is understood that we mean the integral closure of \(R\) in its field of fractions, \(\overline{R}\text{.}\)
