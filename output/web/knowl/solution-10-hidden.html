Indeed, \(\sqrt{d}\) satisfies the monic polynomial \(x^2-d\text{,}\) and since the integral closure of \(\mathbb{Z}\) is a ring containing \(\mathbb{Z}\) and \(\sqrt{d}\text{,}\) and \(\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{d}]\) is the smallest such ring,[^1] we conclude that every element in \(\Z[\sqrt{d}]\) is integral over \(\mathbb{Z}\text{.}\)
