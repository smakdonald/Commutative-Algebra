Proof

Let \(R=A[\Lambda]\text{,}\) with \(\lambda\) integral over \(A\) for all \(\lambda\in\Lambda\text{.}\) Given \(r\in R\text{,}\) there is a finite subset \(L\subseteq \Lambda\) such that \(r\in A[L]\text{.}\) This \(A[L]\) is now a finitely-generated algebra generated by integral elements, and thus it must be module-finite over \(A\)[^2]. Module-finite implies integral[^1], and thus \(A[L]\) is an integral extension of \(A\text{.}\) In particular, \(r \in A[L]\) is integral over \(A\text{.}\)
