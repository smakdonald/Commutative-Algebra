Lemma 5.42. Localization Commutes with Finite Intersections.

Let \(M\) be a module, and \(N_1,\dots,N_t\) be a finite collection of submodules. Let \(W\) be a multiplicative set. Then,
\begin{equation*} W^{-1} (N_1 \cap \cdots \cap N_t) = W^{-1} N_1 \cap \cdots \cap W^{-1} N_t \subseteq W^{-1}M. \end{equation*}
##### Proof. The containment \(W^{-1} (N_1 \cap \cdots \cap N_t) \subseteq W^{-1} N_1 \cap \cdots \cap W^{-1} N_t\) is clear[^1]. Elements of \(W^{-1} N_1 \cap \cdots \cap W^{-1} N_t\) are of the form \(\frac{n_1}{w_1}= \cdots = \frac{n_t}{w_t}\text{;}\) we can find a common denominator to realize this in \(W^{-1} (N_1 \cap \cdots \cap N_t)\text{.}\)
