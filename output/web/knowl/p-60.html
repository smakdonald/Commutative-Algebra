There are many basic questions about algebra generators that are surprisingly difficult. Let \(R=\C[x_1,\dots,x_n]\) and \(f_1,\dots,f_n\in R\text{.}\) When do \(f_1,\dots,f_n\) generate \(R\) over \(\C\text{?}\) It is not too hard to show that the Jacobian determinant
\begin{equation*} \det \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial f_1}{\partial x_1} & \cdots & \frac{\partial f_1}{\partial x_n} \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \frac{\partial f_n}{\partial x_1} & \cdots & \frac{\partial f_n}{\partial x_n} \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
must be a nonzero constant. It is a big open question whether this is in fact a sufficient condition!
