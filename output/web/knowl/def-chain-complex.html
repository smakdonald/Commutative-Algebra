Definition 1.1. Chain Complex.

A chain complex of \(R\)-modules \(\left(C_{\bullet}, \delta_{\bullet}\right)\text{,}\) also referred to simply as a complex, is a sequence of \(R\)-modules \(C_{i}\) and \(R\)-module homomorphisms
\begin{equation*} \cdots \longrightarrow C_{n+1} \stackrel{\delta_{n+1}}{\longrightarrow} C_{n} \stackrel{\delta_{n}}{\longrightarrow} C_{n-1} \longrightarrow \cdots \end{equation*}
such that \(\delta_{n} \delta_{n+1}=0\) for all \(n\text{.}\) We refer to \(C_{n}\) as the module in homological degree \(n\text{.}\) The maps \(\delta_{n}\) are the differentials of our complex. We may sometimes omit the differentials \(\delta_{n}\) and simply refer to the complex \(C_{\bullet}\) or even \(C\text{;}\) we may also sometimes refer to \(\delta_{\bullet}\) as the differential of \(C_{\text {. }}\text{.}\)
