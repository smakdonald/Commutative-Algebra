Zerodivisors Associated Primes ##### Statement If \(R\) is noetherian, and \(M\) is an arbitrary \(R\)-module, then
\begin{equation*} \bigcup\limits_{P \in \Ass(M)} P = \mathcal{Z}(M). \end{equation*}
##### Proof. If \(r \in \mathcal{Z}(M)\text{,}\) then by definition we have \(r \in \ann(m)\) for some nonzero \(m \in M\text{.}\) Since \(\ann(m)\) is contained in some associated prime of \(M\text{,}\) then \(r\) is also contained in some associated prime of \(M\text{.}\) On the other hand, if \(P\) is an associated prime of \(M\text{,}\) then by definition all elements in \(P\) are zerodivisors on \(M\text{.}\)
