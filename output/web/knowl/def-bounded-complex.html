Definition 1.3. Bounded.

We will say a complex is bounded above if \(F_{n}=0\) for all \(n \gg 0\text{,}\) and bounded below if \(F_{n}=0\) for all \(n \ll 0\text{.}\) A bounded complex is one that is both bounded above and below. If a complex is bounded, we may sometimes simply write it as a finite complex, say
\begin{equation*} C_{n} \stackrel{\delta_{n}}{\longrightarrow} C_{n-1} \longrightarrow \cdots \longrightarrow C_{m} \end{equation*}
