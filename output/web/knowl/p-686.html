Let \(R = k[x_{11},x_{21},x_{22}, x_{31}, x_{32}, x_{33},\ldots]\) be a polynomial ring in infinitely many variables, which we are thinking of as arranged in an infinite triangle. \(R\) is clearly infinite-dimensional and not noetherian. Let
\begin{equation*} W = R \setminus \big((x_{11}) \cup (x_{21},x_{22}) \cup (x_{31},x_{32},x_{33}) \cup \cdots \big) = \bigcap_{n \geqslant 1} \big(R \setminus (x_{n1},\dots,x_{nn})\big) \end{equation*}
and \(S=W^{-1}R\text{.}\) Note that \(W\) is an intersection of multiplicatively closed subsets, so this is a valid localization of \(R\text{.}\)
