Definition A.5. Ideal.

A nonempty subset \(I\) of a ring \(R\) is an ideal if it is closed for the addition and for multiplication by any element in \(R\text{:}\) for any \(a, b \in I\) and \(r \in R\text{,}\) we must have \(a+b \in I\) and \(ra \in I\text{.}\) The ideal generated by \(f_1, \ldots, f_n\text{,}\) denoted \((f_1, \ldots, f_n)\text{,}\) is the smallest ideal containing \(f_1, \ldots, f_n\text{,}\) or equivalently,
\begin{equation*} (f_1, \ldots, f_n) = \left\lbrace r_1 f_1 + \cdots r_n f_n \, | \, r_i \in R \right\rbrace. \end{equation*}
