Item 1.

Let \(k\) be an infinite field. Consider \(X = \cZ(xy -1) \subseteq \A^2_k\) (i.e., \(X\) is a hyperbola) and define \(\phi: X \to \A^1_k\) by \(\phi(a,b) = a\text{.}\) Then \(\phi\) is an algebraic map (indeed, it is given by a linear polynomial) and its image is \(\A^1_k \setminus \{0\}\text{,}\) which is not an algebraic subset of \(\A^1_k\text{.}\) So the set-theoretic image of a morphism of varieties need not be a variety.
