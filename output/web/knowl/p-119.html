We cannot talk about the integral closure of a ring \(R\) without specifying in what extension; the integral closures of \(R\) in different extension can be very different. In Example 1.41, we saw that the integral closure of \(\Z\) in \(\Z[\sqrt{2}]\) contains at least \(\Z\) and \(\sqrt{2}\text{,}\) while Example 1.44 says that the integral closure of \(\Z\) in \(\Q\) is \(\Z\text{.}\)
