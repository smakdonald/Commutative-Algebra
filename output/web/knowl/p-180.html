Since \(B_0\) is noetherian and \(C\) is a finitely generated \(B_0\)-module, \(C\) is a noetherian \(B_0\)-module, by [[Mathematics/Commutative Algebra/Results/Proposition - Submodule of F.G. Module F.G.|Proposition]]. Since \(B\subseteq C\text{,}\) then \(B\) is also a finitely generated \(B_0\)-module[^3]. In particular, \(B_0 \subseteq B\) is algebra-finite. Since \(A \subseteq B_0\) is algebra-finite, we conclude that \(A \subseteq B\) is algebra-finite, as required[^2].
