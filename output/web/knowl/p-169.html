Suppose that
\begin{equation*} \begin{CD} 0@>>>M@>>>0 \end{CD} \end{equation*}
is an exact sequence. This means that the image of the zero map to \(M\text{,}\) which is the zero module, is the same as the kernel of the zero map from \(M\text{,}\) which is all of \(M\text{.}\) Thus saying that
\begin{equation*} \begin{CD} 0@>>>M@>>>0 \end{CD} \end{equation*}
is exact is equivalent to saying that \(M = 0\text{.}\)
