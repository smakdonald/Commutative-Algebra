The following is a short exact sequence of \(\Z\)-modules:
\begin{equation*} \begin{CD} 0@>>>\Z@>2>>\Z@>>>\Zm 2@>>>0 \end{CD} \end{equation*}
Indeed, multiplication by \(2\) on \(\Z\) is injective, and its cokernel is \(\Zm 2\text{.}\) Another way to look at this is to notice that the kernel of the canonical projection map \(\Z \to \Zm 2\) is the ideal generated by \(2\text{,}\) which is a free \(\Z\)-module with \(1\) generator. The map \(\Z \xrightarrow{\, 2 \,} \Z\) corresponds to the inclusion of that module in \(\Z\text{.}\)
