  1. Let \(k\) be an infinite field. Consider \(X = \cZ(xy -1) \subseteq \A^2_k\) (i.e., \(X\) is a hyperbola) and define \(\phi: X \to \A^1_k\) by \(\phi(a,b) = a\text{.}\) Then \(\phi\) is an algebraic map (indeed, it is given by a linear polynomial) and its image is \(\A^1_k \setminus \{0\}\text{,}\) which is not an algebraic subset of \(\A^1_k\text{.}\) So the set-theoretic image of a morphism of varieties need not be a variety.
  2. Take an infinite field, and let \(Y\) be the classical cuspidal curve:
    \begin{equation*} Y = \cZ(y^2 - x^3) \subseteq \A^2_k. \end{equation*}
    Define
    \begin{equation*} \phi: \A^1_K \to Y \quad \phi(t) = (t^2, t^3). \end{equation*}
