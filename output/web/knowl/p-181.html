Let \(M\) be an \(R\)-module. The following are equivalent:
  1. \(M\) is a noetherian module.
  2. Every nonempty family of submodules has a maximal element.
  3. Every ascending chain of finitely generated submodules of \(M\) eventually stabilizes.
  4. Given any generating set \(S\) for a submodule \(N\text{,}\) the submodule \(N\) is generated by a finite subset of \(S\text{.}\)
  5. Every submodule of \(M\) is finitely generated. In particular, a noetherian module must be finitely generated.
