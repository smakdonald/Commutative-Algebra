Associated Primes Localize in Noetherian Rings ##### Statement Let \(R\) be a noetherian ring, \(W\) a multiplicative set, and \(M\) a module. Then
\begin{equation*} \Ass_{W^{-1}R}(W^{-1}M)=\{W^{-1}P \ | \ P \in \Ass_R(M), \, P \cap W = \varnothing\}. \end{equation*}
##### Proof. Given \(P \in \Ass_R(M)\) such that \(P \cap W= \varnothing\text{,}\) [provisional cross-reference: cite] [[Mathematics/Commutative Algebra/Results/Lemma - Localization Ideals|Lemma]] says that \(W^{-1}P\) is a prime in \(W^{-1}R\text{.}\) Then \(W^{-1}R /W^{-1}P \cong W^{-1}(R/P) \hookrightarrow W^{-1}M\) by exactness, so \(W^{-1}P\) is an associated prime of \(W^{-1} M\text{.}\)
