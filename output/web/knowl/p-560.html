The assumption says that[^1]
\begin{equation*} I\subseteq \displaystyle\bigcup_{P \in \Ass(M)}(P). \end{equation*}
By the assumptions, [[Mathematics/Commutative Algebra/Results/Corollary - Ass Finite|Corollary]] applies, and it guarantees that this is a finite set of primes. By [[Mathematics/Commutative Algebra/Results/Lemma - Prime Avoidance|prime avoidance]], \(I \subseteq P\) for some \(P \in \Ass(M)\text{.}\) Equivalently, \(I\subseteq \ann_R(m)\)[^2] for some nonzero \(m\in M\text{.}\)[^3]
