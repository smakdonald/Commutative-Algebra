Fix an ideal \(I\) and a prime \(P \supseteq I\text{,}\) and consider the set
\begin{equation*} S = \lbrace Q \in V(I) \mid P \supseteq Q \rbrace, \end{equation*}
which is partially ordered with \(\supseteq\text{.}\) On the one hand, \(P \in S\text{,}\) so \(S\) is nonempty. On the other hand, given any chain \(\{ Q_i \}_i\) in \(S\text{,}\) \(Q := \bigcap_i Q_i\) is a prime ideal in \(R\) ( #prove !). Moreover, \(Q\) contains \(I\text{,}\) since every \(Q_i\) contain \(I\text{,}\) and \(Q\) is contained in \(P\text{,}\) since every \(P_i \subseteq Q\text{.}\) Therefore, \(Q \in S\text{,}\) and Zorn's Lemma applies to \(S\text{.}\) By [provisional cross-reference: cite] [[Mathematics/Foundations/Results/Theorem - Zorn's Lemma]], \(S\) contains a maximal element for \(\supseteq\text{,}\) say \(Q\text{.}\)
