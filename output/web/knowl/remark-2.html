Remark 1.18.

Let \(S\) be an \(R\)-algebra generated by \(\Lambda\text{,}\) let \(\pi\) be the surjective map in part (3) of this [provisional cross-reference: cite] [[Lemma - Generated Algebra Equivalencies|Lemma]], and let \(I := \ker \pi\text{.}\) By the [provisional cross-reference: cite] [[Theorem - First Isomorphism Theorem#$ thm$ - First Isomorphism Theorem for Rings|First Isomorphism Theorem]], we have a ring isomorphism \(S\cong R[X]/I\text{.}\) The elements of \(I\) are the relations among the generators in \(\Lambda\text{.}\) If we understand the ring \(R\) and generators and relations for \(S\) over \(R\text{,}\) we can get a pretty concrete understanding of \(S\text{.}\)
